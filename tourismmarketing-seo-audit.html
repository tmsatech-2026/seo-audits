On-Page SEO Issues

The elements on each page that signal relevance to Google and convince searchers to click through from results.

Critical Missing Meta Descriptions on Service Pages
Evidence

No meta description found on the homepage, services page, SEO page, social media page, or contact page. Blog posts like the 2025 social media trends article do have descriptions (good), but the core commercial pages do not.

Why It Matters

Your service pages are the ones that need to convert. Without a compelling meta description, Google auto-generates snippets that are often pulled from random page content, reducing click-through rates.

Recommendation

Write unique meta descriptions (150-160 chars) for every service page using Rank Math. Include the primary keyword, your niche differentiator (hospitality focus), and a call to action.

Critical Legacy .php Pages Still Indexed by Google
Evidence

Google search results show old pages like /marketing_e-mail_branding.php, /marketing-social-media-hospitality-industry.php, /internet_hosting.php, and even a PDF (/services/TMSA_Info_Guest_Centre.pdf). These are from a previous version of the site and may show outdated content or styling.

Why It Matters

Prospects who find these old pages get a poor first impression. It also splits your ranking authority between old and new versions of the same content.

Recommendation
  • 301 redirect every legacy .php URL to its corresponding modern page
  • e.g., /marketing_e-mail_branding.php → /email-marketing/
  • e.g., /marketing-social-media-hospitality-industry.php → /social-media/
  • Remove or restrict access to the PDF in /services/
Critical Outdated SEO Page Title: "#1 SEO Services In 2021"
Evidence

The SEO service page title is "#1 SEO / Search Engine Optimisation Services In 2021". It's now 2026. This signals to both Google and prospects that the page hasn't been maintained in 5 years.

Recommendation

Update to something evergreen like "SEO for Guest Houses, Hotels & Lodges | TMSA" -- or if using a year, update it annually. Check all service page titles for similar dating issues.

High Thin Service Pages (Form-Heavy, Content-Light)
Evidence

Service pages (/seo-marketing/, /social-media/, /website-design/, /email-marketing/, etc.) have minimal content depth. Most rely heavily on visual design and contact forms rather than detailed explanations of your approach, process, or results.

Recommendation
  • Expand each service page to 1,000-1,500+ words of genuine value
  • Include: what you do, your process, who it's for, expected results, FAQ section
  • Add hospitality-specific examples and terminology to reinforce your niche expertise
High Missing Image Alt Text Across the Site
Evidence

Images across the site lack descriptive alt text, including portfolio property photos, service icons, and blog post images.

Recommendation

Add descriptive alt text to every image. For portfolio properties, use the property name and location. For blog images, describe the topic visually. This improves both SEO and accessibility.

High Missing Open Graph & Social Meta Tags
Evidence

No Open Graph or Twitter Card meta tags detected. As an agency that offers social media marketing, your own pages should display beautifully when shared.

Recommendation

Enable OG tags and Twitter Cards in Rank Math's social settings. Create branded sharing images (1200x630px) for all key pages and blog posts.

Medium Sitemap Not Referenced in Robots.txt
Evidence

Your robots.txt only contains User-agent and Disallow rules. There's no Sitemap: directive pointing to your sitemap_index.xml.

Recommendation

Add "Sitemap: https://www.tourismmarketing.co.za/sitemap_index.xml" to your robots.txt. This helps search engines discover and crawl all your content faster.
