01 — Technical SEO

Housekeeping tasks to ensure search engines can discover, index, and correctly understand the updated content. Most are one-time setup actions with outsized long-term impact.

HIGH
Submit sitemap to Google Search Console
Log in to Google Search Console, go to Sitemaps, and submit kunjani-villas.co.za/sitemap_index.xml (the Rank Math sitemap URL). This signals all updated pages to Google and accelerates re-indexing of the new meta titles and descriptions.
HIGH
Request indexing for key pages individually
In Google Search Console, use URL Inspection → Request Indexing for the highest-value pages: homepage, each villa page, contact, and booking enquiry. This fast-tracks the new meta data appearing in search results rather than waiting for the next crawl cycle.
HIGH
Add LodgingBusiness schema markup
Add JSON-LD schema of type LodgingBusiness (or VacationRental) to the homepage and key villa pages. Include: name, description, address, telephone, priceRange, amenityFeature, photo, checkInTime, checkOutTime. This helps both Google and LLMs like ChatGPT accurately describe the property in AI-generated results.
HIGH
Verify Google Analytics 4 conversion events
Confirm GA4 is tracking meaningful events: booking enquiry submissions, phone clicks, WhatsApp link taps, and contact form completions. Without conversion tracking, it is impossible to measure the ROI of SEO or paid ads. Use GA4 DebugView to test each event.
MEDIUM
Check Core Web Vitals in Search Console
Review the Core Web Vitals report in Search Console (Experience → Core Web Vitals). Poor scores — particularly Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) — are a direct ranking factor. For image-heavy hospitality sites, the main culprit is usually hero images that are not WebP-optimised or lazy-loaded.
MEDIUM
Audit internal linking structure
Ensure each villa page links to related villa pages and the main booking/enquiry page. Blog posts (once published) should link to the most relevant villa page using descriptive anchor text. Strong internal linking distributes page authority and keeps visitors engaged — both positive SEO signals.
MEDIUM
Set up Bing Webmaster Tools
Submit the site to Bing Webmaster Tools (webmaster.bing.com). Bing powers both Bing Search and DuckDuckGo, and — importantly — is a key data source behind Microsoft Copilot (AI chat). Submitting your sitemap here significantly improves visibility beyond Google, including in AI-assisted search.
LOW
Review image alt text across the site
Now that meta titles and descriptions are optimised, the natural next step is ensuring all property photos have descriptive alt text (e.g. "Private pool at Kunjani Villas with vineyard views, Stellenbosch"). Alt text contributes to image search rankings and improves accessibility scores, which Google uses as a quality signal.
02 — Content Strategy: What Makes Great Blog Content

Well-written blog content is the single highest-ROI long-term SEO investment. It attracts organic traffic, earns backlinks, and trains the LLMs powering AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) to cite and recommend the property.

SEO
Write long-form, question-answering articles
Target 1,500–2,500 words per article. Each post should answer a specific question a potential guest would type into Google — e.g. "best private villa Cape Winelands for a family holiday". Long-form content ranks higher, earns more backlinks, and is more likely to be cited by AI search engines.
SEO
Use clear H2/H3 headings and FAQ sections
Use H2 subheadings for each main point and a FAQ section at the end of every post. FAQs dramatically increase the chance of winning featured snippets in Google and are the primary way LLMs extract answers about a property. Example: "Can I bring children to Kunjani Villas?" → direct, factual answer.
SEO
Link every blog post to a key booking page
Each post should include at least two internal links to villa pages or the booking enquiry form with natural anchor text (e.g. "book The Garden Villa directly"). This passes SEO authority to commercial pages and keeps readers moving towards a booking rather than bouncing off the site.
LLM
Write factually and specifically about the property
LLMs like ChatGPT and Perplexity learn about properties from on-site content. Include specific details throughout: guest capacity, villa names, distances to nearby wine estates, what is included, check-in times, pool features. The more specific and factual the content, the more confidently an AI will describe and recommend the property.
SEO
Include original photos with descriptive alt text
Include 3–6 original photos per post (not stock), each with descriptive alt text. Images increase time-on-page, reduce bounce rate, and appear in Google Image Search. For hospitality, visual content is often the deciding factor between a visitor who enquires and one who leaves.
SEO
Publish consistently — 2 posts per month minimum
Consistency matters more than volume. Two well-researched posts per month (24 per year) will build topical authority faster than sporadic bursts. Prioritise evergreen content so posts remain relevant and attract traffic for years, not weeks. Each new post is a new entry point into the site from Google.

06 — Google Ads: Driving Qualified Paid Traffic

While SEO builds long-term organic visibility, Google Ads delivers immediate, targeted traffic. For a villa property, three campaign types provide the strongest return: brand protection, generic accommodation search, and remarketing.

HIGH
Branded search campaign — protect the brand name
Run a low-budget campaign targeting exact searches for "Kunjani Villas" and close variants. This prevents competitors or OTAs from appearing above the official website when potential guests search the property by name. Branded clicks are inexpensive (low competition) and convert at the highest rate of any keyword type — typically 5–10% vs 1–2% for generic terms.
HIGH
Generic search campaign — winelands accommodation keywords
Target high-intent terms: "cape winelands villa rental", "private villa stellenbosch", "luxury accommodation winelands", "self-catering winelands". Use responsive search ads with 5–8 headline variants. Target geographically: Cape Town metro, Johannesburg/Pretoria, and key international markets (UK, Germany, Netherlands, Australia). Suggested starting budget: ZAR 3,000–8,000/month.
MEDIUM
Remarketing campaign — re-engage visitors who did not book
Install the Google Ads remarketing pixel on the website. Create a Display remarketing audience of users who visited villa pages or the enquiry page but did not complete a booking. Show them targeted ads for 30 days after their visit. Remarketing audiences are 10× cheaper to advertise to and convert at 2–3× the rate of cold traffic — the highest-ROI paid channel for accommodation businesses.
