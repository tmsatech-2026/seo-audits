On-Page SEO Issues

These are the elements on each page that tell Google what it's about and convince searchers to click. This is where the biggest quick wins are.

Critical Missing Meta Descriptions on All Pages
Evidence

No meta description tag was found on any of the 5 pages. Google is auto-generating snippets from page content, which are often inaccurate and uncompelling.

Why It Matters

Meta descriptions are your "ad copy" in search results. A well-written description can increase click-through rates by 5-10%, meaning more traffic from the same ranking positions.

Recommendation

Write a unique, compelling meta description (150-160 characters) for every page using the Yoast SEO plugin that is already installed. Each description should include the primary keyword and a clear call to action.

Critical Generic, Non-Descriptive Title Tags
Evidence

All pages use the pattern "[PageName] - Rooms Online" (e.g., "Home - Rooms Online", "Services - Rooms Online"). These contain no target keywords and don't describe what the business does.

Why It Matters

Title tags are the single most important on-page ranking factor. Google uses them to understand what a page is about. Generic titles mean Google doesn't know which searches to show your pages for.

Recommendation

Rewrite every title to include target keywords. See the Title Tag Recommendations section below for specific suggestions per page.

Critical Weak Heading Structure (H1 Tags)
Evidence

H1 tags use generic labels ("Home", "Services", "Portfolio") rather than descriptive, keyword-rich headings. Sub-headings (H2, H3) are either missing or used for styling rather than content hierarchy.

Why It Matters

Headings help Google understand your content structure and topic relevance. They also improve accessibility and user experience.

Recommendation
  • Homepage H1: "Maximize Your Property's Online Revenue"
  • Services H1: "Hospitality Revenue Management & Distribution Services"
  • Portfolio H1: "Properties We've Helped Succeed Across Southern Africa"
  • Use H2s and H3s to create clear content hierarchy within each page
High Missing Image Alt Text
Evidence

Images across the site, including the logo and partner logos, lack descriptive alt text. Image file names are generic (e.g., "ROL-Logo.png").

Recommendation

Add descriptive alt text to every image. Use natural language that describes what the image shows and includes relevant keywords where appropriate. Rename image files to be descriptive (e.g., "rooms-online-hospitality-management-logo.png").

High Missing Open Graph & Social Meta Tags
Evidence

No Open Graph (og:) or Twitter Card meta tags were detected. When your pages are shared on social media or messaging apps, they appear without a preview image or description.

Recommendation

Configure Yoast SEO's social settings to add OG tags and Twitter Cards to every page. Create a branded social sharing image (1200x630px) for a professional appearance when links are shared.

Medium No Contact Details Visible (NAP)
Evidence

The Contact page has a form but no visible phone number, email address, or physical address. NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency is a fundamental trust and local SEO signal.

Recommendation

Add your full business name, address, phone number, and email to both the Contact page and the site footer. This builds trust with visitors and supports local SEO signals.
